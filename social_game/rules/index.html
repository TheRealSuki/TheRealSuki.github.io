Game Rules

Follow these sacred rules to ensure a fair and thrilling hunt.

The Story So Far...

A creeping darkness has fallen over our once-peaceful town. Mysterious forces are at play, and whispers in the shadows suggest that one of our own has been possessed by a powerful Demon! This foul entity works from the inside, aided by secret accomplices who walk among us during the day. Trust no one. The fate of the town rests in your hands.

Getting Started

At the beginning of the game, each player secretly receives a character token from the bag. You must read your role and ability without showing it to anyone else. Your role determines your team and your special powers.

The Phases of the Game

1. Day Phase

During the day, all living players can openly discuss who they suspect is evil. You are free to share information (or lies!) with the group. Players may also request private conversations with others, away from the main group, to share secrets or coordinate strategies.

2. Voting Phase

Any player may choose to nominate another player for execution. Once a player is nominated, all living players vote. If the nominee receives more than half of the votes, they are marked for execution. However, the nomination process can continue. If a subsequent nominee gets even more votes, they become the new player to be executed. The player with the most votes (and more than half) at the end of the day is executed.

3. Night Phase

All players close their eyes and go to "sleep". The Game Master will then wake individual players one by one, prompting them to use their nightly abilities with hand signals. To prevent players from hearing who is woken up, music will be played. The Game Master will also move around the room to obscure the positions of active players.

Factions & How to Win

There are three factions in the game: The Good Team (Townsfolk & Outsiders), and The Evil Team (Demon & Minions).

  • The Good Team wins if the Demon is executed by a vote. Even if you are an Outsider, you are on the Good team (unless a role ability says otherwise).
  • The Evil Team wins when the number of evil players is equal to the number of good players, or when nothing can prevent this from happening. The Imp may kill one player each night to work towards this goal.

Important Notes

  • Dead Players: When you die, you are not out of the game! You must keep your eyes closed at night. During the day, you may still talk, but you only have one final vote you can use for the rest of the game.
  • Bluffing: Any player can claim to be any role at any time. Deception is a key part of the game.
  • Game Master: If you have any questions about your role or the rules, you can always ask for a private chat with the Game Master.
